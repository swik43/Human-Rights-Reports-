# Country: Bangladesh

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Bangladesh’s location and high population density make it one of the world’s most disaster-prone countries. Disasters are the main trigger of displacement in the country, particularly during the June-September monsoon season when floods displace an average of a million people each year. Cyclones displace an average of 110,000 a year.


A low intensity monsoon season and the absence of destructive storms led to a decrease in new disaster displacements in 2021, when 99,000 were recorded, more than three-quarters of them during the monsoon season. Cyclone Yaas struck the Bay of Bengal in May and triggered 18,000 displacements, the only storm of the year to force people from their homes. Though this is significantly fewer than in previous years, it is to be expected given that severe cyclones tend to hit Bangladesh every two or three years.


Despite the lower figures, the risk of secondary displacement and prolonged humanitarian needs is still high because disasters affect the same areas each year. Yaas, for example, affected Khulna and Barisal divisions, where Cyclone Amphan had triggered 2.5 million displacements in May 2020. At least 15,000 people were still displaced in Khulna as a result of Amphan as of February 2021.


No single agency is responsible for collecting and reporting on disaster displacement in Bangladesh. Consequently, data availability and coverage can change from year to year. The government did, however, adopt a national strategy on the management of climate-induced internal displacement in 2021, which constitutes a significant step towards addressing and preventing disaster displacement.


Political and intercommunal violence trigger localised displacements in Bangladesh every year, but the phenomenon is likely to be under-reported because there are no official sources collecting data on it. Dozens of homes were destroyed in violence between Hindu and Muslim communities in Rangpur district in October. However, most of the 427,000 people who were still displaced by conflict and violence as of the end of 2021 in Bangladesh have been displaced for decades, though updated and reliable data is hard to come by. The majority fled conflict that took place in the Chittagong Hill Tracts from the 1970s until 1997 between government forces and Shanti Bahini, the armed wing of the indigenous peoples’ political party, Parbatya Chattagram Jana Samhati Samiti (PCJSS). Around 275,000 people were still displaced in the region as of 2009.
