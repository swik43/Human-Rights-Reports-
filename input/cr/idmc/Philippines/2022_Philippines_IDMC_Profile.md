# Country: Philippines

---

## Year: 2022

### Country Profile

The Philippines sits in East Asia's typhoon belt and on the Pacific Ring of Fire. It is highly prone to storms and floods that displace millions of people every year, and regular earthquakes and volcanic activity trigger significant displacement as well. Conflict and violence also force people from their homes every year in the southern region of Mindanao, where thousands of people live in protracted displacement as a result.


Disasters triggered 5.4 million internal displacements in 2022. Storms caused 85 per cent of disaster displacements. Tropical storm Nalgae, known locally as Paeng, was the most significant event. It cut across ten provinces between 26 and 30 October, triggering around three million displacements, the highest figure for a single event in the East Asia and Pacific region in 2022. Most were pre-emptive evacuations led by the government, which set up nearly 10,700 evacuation centres to host those displaced. A total of 55,000 people were still displaced as a result of the storm at the end of 2022.


Another significant event was tropical storm Megi, known locally as Agaton, which triggered over 871,000 displacements in April 2022. This storm was significant as it hit the same areas affected in December 2021 by typhoon Rai, namely Caraga, Western Visayas and Central Visayas, which together accounted for nearly 80 per cent of the movements triggered by Megi. Around 40 per cent of the region’s returnees from Rai were forced to flee again.


The recurrence of storms and other weather-related hazards has prompted authorities to strengthen their monitoring systems. Data from the Disaster Response Operations Monitoring and Information Centre (DROMIC) enables more comprehensive understanding of displacement trends, including the pace of returns. In Western Visayas, 95 per cent of IDPs displaced by Rai had returned to their homes in 21 days. A specific rehabilitation and recovery plan was implemented for the region given the scale of displacement and it was instrumental in ensuring swift response to the disaster. Only 1,200 people were still displaced as of mid-October 2022.


Other triggers included two powerful earthquakes that hit Abra province within a period of three months. The first, of magnitude 7.0, occurred on 27 July 2022 and the second, of magnitude 6.4, on 25 October 2022. Together, they triggered around 205,000 displacements and left 50,000 people still living in displacement at the end 2022.


Conflict and violence triggered around 123,000 displacements in 2022, 91 per cent of which took place in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. There, the total number of IDPs fell from 445,300 in 2017 to 102,000 at the end of 2022 due to the return of most of the people who had fled the 2017 battle in Marawi city. Still, nearly 80 per cent of the country’s remaining conflict IDPs as of end of 2022 were displaced by that event.
