# Country: Sudan

---

## Year: 2022

### Country Profile

In 2022, most violence in Sudan, in particular in Darfur, stemmed from intercommunal disputes over land, grazing routes, and other resources. The transitional government and several non-state armed groups (NSAGs) signed the Juba Peace Agreement (JPA) in October 2020, with the aim of tackling key issues such as land, IDPs’ return, transitional justice, security sector reform and political representation.


Implementation, however, has faced challenges and delays. Cattle-herding communities in Darfur in particular feared being evicted if the traditional system of land ownership was fully restored and IDPs returned to what used to be their land, as envisaged in the JPA.


The withdrawal of the United Nations-African Union Hybrid Operation in Darfur in June 2021 also left a security vacuum that reduced protection for civilians.


Violent clashes over resources continued in 2022, triggering 314,000 displacements. Most were recorded in West Darfur. While the figure is a reduction from the 442,000 movements reported in 2021, it is significantly higher than the annual average reported between 2015 and 2020.


Conflict and violence left around 3.6 million people living in internal displacement across the country as of the end of 2022. Over a million live in camp sites in South Darfur, some of them for over a decade. Options to return are limited as land was repurposed or reappropriated during their absence. In addition, insecurity hampers many from returning.


As political instability and unresolved intercommunal tensions persist, disaster risk reduction and resilience strategies have stalled, leaving IDPs particularly vulnerable to disasters, especially flooding. South Darfur was the state most affected by floods in 2022, which destroyed agricultural land, killed livestock and damaged housing and infrastructure, sometimes obstructing people in need from accessing aid. Heavy rains started in June, causing floods that continued for months. By August, the government had declared a state of emergency across a third of the country’s states. The floods triggered around 103,000 internal displacements countrywide, a slightly higher figure from 2021, but less than a quarter of the devastating 2020 floods. By the end of 2022, close to 227,000 people were living in displacement as a result of disasters.
