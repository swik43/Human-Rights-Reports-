# Country: Burkina Faso

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Burkina Faso is in the throes one of the largest internal displacement crises in Africa, along with neighbouring Mali and Niger. The situation has its roots in a complex set of factors including poverty, inequality and the growing presence and activities of non-state armed groups (NSAGs).  Intercommunal clashes over land, water and other scarce resources have also become more common. NSAGs have exploited these disputes and the marginalisation of certain communities to expand their influence and territorial reach.


The number of IDPs increased more than thirty-fold, from 47,000 in 2018 to 1.6 million at the end of 2021, making the situation one of the world’s fastest-growing displacement crises. Conflict and violence triggered 682,000 internal displacements last year alone, more than in Mali and Niger combined. Violence against civilians increased, and many families fled their homes pre-emptively for fear of being attacked by NSAGs. An armed assault on Solhan in the northern region of Sahel in June resulted in at least 160 fatalities, the highest number from a single attack in recent years, and triggered more than 7,000 displacements, 4,800 of them involving children.


The most significant displacement of the year occurred at the end of November and into December when a series of major attacks prompted more than 192,000 new movements in a matter of weeks. NSAGs also extended their activities southwards into previously unaffected areas such as the Sud-Ouest and Cascades regions on the border with Benin and Côte d’Ivoire. Protests against the government’s response to the growing insecurity erupted across the country’s major cities, and the military seized power in early 2022.


The crisis has disproportionately affected women and children, who make up nearly 80 per cent of the country’s IDPs. More than 70 per cent of households to have fled their homes have been displaced for more than a year. The conflict and violence show no signs of abating and IDPs’ prospects of achieving durable solutions are bleak. 


Environmental degradation and climate variability also drive displacement risk in Burkina Faso. The combination of disasters and conflict have also caused acute levels of food insecurity in the Sahel, Nord, Centre-Nord and Est regions, which host most of the country’s IDPs.
