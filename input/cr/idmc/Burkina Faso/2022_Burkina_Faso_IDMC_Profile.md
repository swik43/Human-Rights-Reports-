# Country: Burkina Faso

---

## Year: 2022

### Country Profile

Burkina Faso is in the throes of one of the largest internal displacement crises in Africa. The situation has its roots in a complex set of factors including poverty, inequality and the growing activities of non-state armed groups (NSAGs). Intercommunal clashes over land, water and other resources have also become more common. NSAGs have exploited these disputes and the marginalisation of certain communities to expand their influence and territorial reach.


Conflict and violence triggered 438,000 internal displacements in 2022, a reduction from the 2021 figure in part due to humanitarian access constraints that hampered data collection. The number of violent incidents rose seven-fold between 2018 and 2022, the number of food-insecure people nine-fold and the number of IDPs 30-fold. At the end of the year, around 1.9 million people were living in internal displacement.


The number of areas with severe access constraints increased in the second half of the year. NSAGs destroyed transport infrastructure and attacked relief convoys to loot them or deter others from being sent. They also prevented water trucks from reaching people in need, contaminated wells, destroyed water facilities and sabotaged water generators during the dry season, affecting almost 840,000 people. As a result, less food and other aid were delivered, sanitation conditions deteriorated and agricultural production dropped. A quarter of the country’s population, or around 5.7 million people, were in need of humanitarian aid as of August 2022, an increase of 40 per cent in six months.


A blockade on Djibo, the capital of Soum province, has been in place since February 2022, which has severely interrupted supply lines, impeded humanitarian access and increased food insecurity for all inhabitants. Djibo has received more than 250,000 IDPs since 2019, making up more than three-quarters of the city’s population.


Little data on disaster displacement is available. In 2022, floods triggered 2,400 internal displacements in Boucle du Mouhoun, but figures should be considered a significant underestimate. Humanitarian partners are starting to collect disaster displacement data, which should provide a better picture of the scope and scale of the phenomenon moving forward.
