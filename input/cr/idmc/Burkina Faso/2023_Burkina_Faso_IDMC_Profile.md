# Country: Burkina Faso

---

## Year: 2023

### Country Profile

Conflict-related internal displacement in Burkina Faso has its roots in a complex set of factors including poverty, inequality and the growing activities of non-state armed groups (NSAGs).  NSAGs have exploited disputes over resources and the marginalisation of communities to expand their influence and territorial reach.


In 2023, the country recorded most conflict displacements in the broader West Africa region with 707,000, up 61 per cent from 2022 and the highest figure since the escalation of conflict in 2019. Movements were reported across all regions, the result of a rise in fighting between government forces and several NSAGs, in particular Jama‘at Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin (JNIM), an al-Qaeda affiliate, and the Islamic State in the Sahel, both of which operate in the Liptako-Gourma area along the border between Burkina Faso, Mali and Niger.


Most displacements took place in the northern regions of Centre-Nord, Est and Boucle du Mouhoun, where NSAGs continued to exert control over local people, including by restricting their movement. In the Sahel region also, the number of communes under blockade doubled in 2023, disrupting markets and fuelling food insecurity and malnutrition.


Almost 2.1 million people were living in displacement as a result of conflict and violence as of March 2023, when data last became available. That is a 268 per cent increase on the figure for 2019, but it is most likely an underestimate given that violence and new displacement continued unabated for the rest of 2023.


In terms of disasters, floods triggered over 24,000 internal displacements at the end of August and beginning of September in the Sahel and Est regions. Figures should be considered a significant underestimate as data collection was constrained by insecurity.
