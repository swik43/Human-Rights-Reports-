# Country: China

---

## Year: 2022

### Country Profile

China records some of the highest numbers of disaster displacements globally each year. This is linked to its high exposure to a wide range of hazards, including floods, cyclones and earthquakes. The country’s social and economic transformation has also been marked by significant economic growth and development, rural to urban migration and urbanisation. Many densely populated urban areas are in or near exposed coastal areas and river basins as a result, all of which increases the risk of future displacement.


Disasters triggered around 3.6 million internal displacements in 2022, 40 per cent less than in the previous year when figures where the highest since 2016. Flooding accounted for nearly 1.7 million, making China the fourth country with the highest number of flood-related displacements worldwide. Flooding during East Asia’s monsoon season between May and September accounted for almost two-thirds of the total.


Storms, mostly tropical cyclones, triggered 1.8 million movements. The most affected provinces were Zhejiang, Guangdong and Guangxi. Typhoon Muifa was the event triggering most internal displacements in the country in 2022, with 1.3 million in Zhejiang alone, all of which were pre-emptive evacuations as the storm approached.


The country’s high exposure to major storms has prompted authorities to set up early warning systems and evacuation protocols that have successfully reduced the number of people injured and killed by disasters. Pre-emptive evacuations accounted for 72 per cent of all disaster displacements in China in 2022.


The frequency and intensity of weather-related hazards, including floods, droughts and heatwaves are expected to increase, which may in turn increase displacement risk. Given that some areas of China are also prone to earthquakes and tsunamis, resilient infrastructure and housing are particularly needed to avert future displacement.
