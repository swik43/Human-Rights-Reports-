# Country: China

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

China records some of the highest numbers of disaster displacements globally each year. This is linked to its high exposure to a wide range of hazards, including floods, cyclones and earthquakes. The country’s social and economic transformation has also been marked by exponential economic growth and development, rural to urban migration and rapid urbanisation. Many densely populated urban areas are in or near exposed coastal areas and river basins as a result, all of which increases the risk of future displacement.


Disasters triggered around six million internal displacements in 2021, 19 per cent more than in the previous year and the highest figure since 2016. Flooding accounted for 3.8 million, making China the country with the highest number of flood-related displacements worldwide.


Unprecedented rainfall in Henan province between 16 and 31 July caused floods which alone triggered almost 1.5 million new movements. In Zhengzhou, 201.9 mm fell in an hour on 20 July, thought to be the heaviest rain in 1,000 years. Tropical cyclones Cempaka and In-Fa, which made landfall in other parts of China between 20 and 23 July, triggered 105,000 and 1.4 million displacements respectively.


Around 800,000 disaster displacements were also reported outside the May to September rainy season, of which 590,000 took place predominantly in the provinces of Shanxi and Shaanxi in October as rainfall exceeded historical records in some areas.


Many displacements were pre-emptive evacuations led by authorities to avert injury and loss of life. The government has made increasing efforts to improve its management of disaster risk, including the production of disaster loss data. The fact that China consistently records some of the highest disaster displacement figures in the world is in part because of its strength in this area.


The frequency and intensity of weather-related hazards, including floods, droughts and heatwaves are expected to increase, which may in turn increase displacement risk. Given that some areas of China are also prone to earthquakes and tsunamis, resilient infrastructure and housing are also needed to avert future displacement.
