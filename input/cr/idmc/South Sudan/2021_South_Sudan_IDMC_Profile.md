# Country: South Sudan

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

South Sudan seceded from Sudan in 2011 after nearly two decades of civil war. A political crisis followed, leading to a conflict that erupted in December 2013 and led to more than 1.3 million internal displacements in 2014. A peace agreement was signed in 2015, but the number of IDPs remained high. The conflict escalated again in 2016 and by the following year the country had the highest number of IDPs on record. The government and several non-state armed groups signed a revitalised peace agreement in 2018, but implementation has been slow and insecurity has continued.


In fact conflict and violence triggered 429,000 internal displacements in 2021, the highest figure since 2017. Intercommunal and criminal violence in Warrap state led to 192,000 new movements thorough 2021, and intercommunal violence in Tambura county in Western Equatoria as many as 80,000 between June and September. Many people in Tambura were killed and homes were destroyed. Livelihoods were disrupted and floods rendered roads impassable, hampering humanitarian access. Displacement was also reported in Upper Nile and Jonglei, mainly the result of intercommunal violence.


Across the country as a whole 1.4 million people were living in displacement as a result of conflict and violence at the end of the year, a figure largely unchanged since 2018.


South Sudan also experienced heavy flooding for the third year in a row in 2021, which triggered 506,000 new movements, the highest disaster figure in more than a decade. The floods struck nine of the country’s ten states, of which Jonglei, Warrap and Unity were worst affected. About 100,000 people in Jonglei were still living in displacement after the 2020 floods. Local food production was severely affected because most farms were submerged. The consecutive years of widespread flooding also mean that livelihood opportunities such as planting during the lean season are no longer an option for people in affected areas.


Across the country as a whole 527,000 people were living in displacement as a result of disasters at the end of the year, four times the figure for December 2020, which suggests a trend toward increasingly prolonged displacement.


The combined impacts of violence, disasters and displacement have increased food insecurity across the country. Around 60 per cent of the population was food insecure in 2021, the highest figure since independence. People displaced by floods were also at risk of waterborne diseases, and most who fled conflict were living in circumstances far below the minimum emergency thresholds for survival. IDPs sheltering in the bush and swamps faced the worst conditions. Women and girls face extreme levels of gender-based violence, but much goes unreported and services for survivors are limited. Implementing South Sudan’s peace agreement is essential if these concerning trends are to be reversed and durable solutions achieved.
