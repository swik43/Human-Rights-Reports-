# Country: Niger

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

The triggers and drivers of displacement in Niger often overlap. Changes in rainfall patterns have caused both drought and flash flooding that destroy homes and crops, displacing thousands and deepening food insecurity across the country. This has also resulted in competition for scarce resources that enflames intercommunal tensions between pastoralists and farmers, leading to further displacement.


Clashes between government forces and non-state armed groups (NSAGs) and fighting between NSAGs that has spilled over from neighbouring Burkina Faso, Mali, and Nigeria, have prompted significant displacement in Diffa, Maradi, Tahoua and Tillabéri. The four regions accounted for all of the 110,000 new movements associated with conflict and violence in 2021. The figure was lower than the 136,000 recorded in 2020, but IDPs’ humanitarian needs remained significant.


Intercommunal violence in the south-western regions of Tahoua and Tillabéri, which border Burkina Faso and Mali, triggered 83,000 internal displacements, and attacks against civilians and fighting between NSAGs and the military led to further internal and cross-border movements. Insecurity linked to the activities of Boko Haram and other NSAGs in the Lake Chad basin triggered around 40,000 internal displacements in Diffa.


Intercommunal violence also triggered around 7,000 displacements in Maradi, which has become a bridge of violence between the Lake Chad region and the Liptako Gourma region, two previously distinct zones of instability. NSAGs have taken advantage of intercommunal tensions to carry out attacks and expand their reach across the Sahel.


Around 224,000 people were living in internal displacement as result of conflict and violence at the end of year. The figure is a drop from the 257,000 recorded in 2020, in part the result of government-led returns and the relocation of IDPs to around 300 villages in Diffa. The presence of unexploded ordnance was reported in some return areas. Further relocations are planned in Tillabéri and Tahoua in 2022.


Disasters, particularly flooding, triggered 118,000 displacements in 2021, compared with 267,000 in 2020. Many involved people already displaced by conflict, violence, or disasters in Diffa, Maradi, Tahoua and Tillabéri. Around 40,000 people were living in displacement as a result of disasters at the end of the year.
