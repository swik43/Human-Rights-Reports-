# Country: Niger

---

## Year: 2023

### Country Profile

A total of 181,000 conflict displacements were reported in Niger in 2023, the highest figure since data became available in 2015. As in previous years, most took place in the regions of Diffa, in the Lake Chad basin, and Tillabéri, in the western Liptako-Gourma region.


In Diffa, several non-state armed groups (NSAGs) continued to uproot people from their homes in areas near the border with Nigeria and Chad, triggering more than 44,000 movements. In Tillabéri, which borders Mali and Burkina Faso, NSAG attacks increased in the weeks after the military took over the Government in July and led to around 104,000 displacements. International and regional sanctions, the closure of borders and access restrictions which the de facto authorities imposed in some areas meant many people in need, including IDPs, could not be reached.


Around 347,000 people were living in displacement as a result of conflict and violence at the end of 2023, a figure that should be considered conservative.


In terms of disasters, the rainy season caused significant flooding between July and November. Southern provinces, particularly Maradi, which also suffered from flooding in 2022, registered most damages. The floods destroyed more than 15,000 houses and triggered nearly 95,000 internal displacements. People moved to higher ground, and many stayed with host families, but overcrowding increased the spread of diphtheria. Extensive damage to crops and livestock also threatened food security, leading the authorities to provide food aid to affected communities. By the end of 2023, 32,000 people were estimated to remain displaced by disasters across the country.
