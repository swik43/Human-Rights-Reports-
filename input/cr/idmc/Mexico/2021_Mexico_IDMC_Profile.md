# Country: Mexico

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Mexico is prone to a wide range of weather-related and geophysical hazards, and several states are affected by criminal and other forms of violence, all of which lead to internal displacement. Disasters triggered 19,000 movements in 2021 and violence 29,000, the latter a three-fold increase on the figure for 2020. 


Displacement associated with violence took place across the states of Chiapas, Chihuahua, Durango, Guerrero, Jalisco, Michoacán, Nayarit, Oaxaca, Tamaulipas and Zacatecas. Criminal violence accounted for 95 per cent of the total, and most took place in Michoacán, Guerrero and Chiapas.


Michoacán was most affected with 13,000 movements, more than ten times the figure for 2020. Violence between drug traffickers, particularly clashes between the Cartel Jalisco New Generation and the United Cartels, triggered much of the displacement. Criminal violence also led to 3,600 displacements in Zacatecas and further cases in Nayarit, the first time the phenomenon had been recorded in either state.


Around a quarter of the displacements triggered by violence nationwide involved indigenous populations, which were also affected by intercommunal violence. The latter prompted around 380 displacements among both indigenous and non-indigenous communities in Chiapas and Oaxaca, where incidents included cases of conflict over land.


Around 379,000 people were thought to be living in displacement as a result of violence at the end of the year.


Floods accounted for around 70 per cent of the nearly 19,000 disaster displacements recorded in 2021. The most significant event was in the state of Hidalgo in September, when heavy rains led the Tula river to burst its banks, triggering nearly 10,000 evacuations in the city of Tula.


Hurricanes and tropical storms led to around 4,000 displacements, a significant decrease from the 73,000 recorded in 2020. The 2021 hurricane season was the third most active on record in terms of named storms, but fewer developed into full-blown hurricanes than in 2020. Wildfires, landslides, extreme temperatures and earthquakes triggered a further 1,900 movements.


Chiapas adopted state legislation on internal displacement in 2012, Guerrero in 2014 and Sinaloa in 2020. The federal government has also accelerated efforts to draw up a law on internal displacement since 2018, and the lower house of congress approved draft legislation in September 2020. The same chamber also approved an initiative to reform the penal code and criminalise displacement triggered by violence in 2019. Both initiatives, which would clarify the responsibilities of different authorities and improve prevention and responses to internal displacement, are still under consideration in the senate.
