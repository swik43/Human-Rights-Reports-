# Country: Haiti

---

## Year: 2022

### Country Profile

Haiti is highly vulnerable to a range of natural hazards, including hurricanes, floods and earthquakes. The country has also been struggling with a contracting economy and deepening poverty that were driving instability and insecurity even before the onset of Covid-19. Criminal and gang violence have been on the increase since 2018.


Crime-related violence triggered 106,000 displacements in 2022, where the phenomenon has intensified since the assassination of the country’s president in July 2021. Incidents of violence nearly doubled in 2022 compared to 2021, as gangs gained control of 60 per cent of the country’s capital, Port-au-Prince. The overall displacement figure should be considered an underestimate given that data collection was limited and impeded by insecurity.


The increase in violence in Port-au-Prince also triggered 9,000 displacements to the southern departments of Grand’Anse and Sud, where insecurity and road closures restricted IDPs’ access to humanitarian aid. Around 11,000 IDPs were identified elsewhere in the country, particularly in the northern departments of Artibonite and Centre, which were also affected by localised violence.


There were around 171,000 people living in displacement by conflict and violence at the end of the year, more than a ten-fold increase on the figure for 2021. Approximately 88 per cent were concentrated in the metropolitan area of Port-au-Prince, and some had been displaced for more than a year. Nearly three-quarters were living with host communities rather than in displacement sites, and many were confined to their neighbourhoods with only limited access to goods and services. Gender-based violence continues to be a major concern in Haiti. Cases are significantly underreported, but cases in camps may be a reason behind IDPs avoiding formal sites.


To support the local police in combating insecurity driven by armed gangs, the government requested support through an international specialized armed force. The Secretary-General, in turn, proposed the deployment of a rapid action force.


The number of people displaced by disasters in 2022 decreased more than ten-fold compared to 2021. Aftershocks from the devastating August 2021 earthquake occurred on 24 January 2022 displacing close to 3,600 people. A week later, around 11,000 people were forced to flee due to floods caused by heavy rains in the Ouest region. Some of the same areas had already been affected by the earthquakes.
