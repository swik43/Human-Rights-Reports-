# Country: Haiti

---

## Year: 2023

### Country Profile

Haiti is highly vulnerable to a range of natural hazards, including hurricanes, floods and earthquakes. . Criminal and gang violence have been on the increase since 2018 in a context of contracting economy, deepening poverty and political instability.


Violence in 2023 led to a record 245,000 displacements, more than double the figure for 2022, making it the country reporting the largest number of displacements by crime-related violence globally. Most situations of this magnitude tend to instead be the result of armed conflict.


As violence continued to expand across the country, particularly in the capital of Port-au-Prince, displacement figures rose for the fourth year in a row. The escalating violence and insecurity led to the formation of vigilante groups at the start of 2023 to counter the gangs, but their activities also fuelled displacement. Most movements took place in and around the metropolitan area of Port-au-Prince, at least 80 per cent of which was controlled or influenced by more than 200 gangs. As they expanded their presence outside Ouest department, displacement also increased in Centre and Artibonite.


The scale and duration of displacement aggravated the impacts of the crisis, exhausting people’s capacity to cope. As a result, the proportion of IDPs living with host families dropped in the second half of 2023. More began sheltering in displacement sites, where they were exposed to significant protection issues, including gender-based violence and health risks, such as cholera and other waterborne diseases. The situation was even more precarious in makeshift camps.


Disasters have made the humanitarian situation more complex still. Heavy downpour caused floods and landslides in the first days of June, damaging some makeshift camps and shelters in the suburbs of Cité Soleil, Tabarre and Carrefour, some of the poorest in Port-au-Prince, and triggering 9,100 displacements. The 2023 floods caused more displacements than any previous flooding in the country.


Almost 311,000 people were living in internal displacement as result violence in Haiti at the end of 2023, nearly one hundred fifty times the figure for 2019, when such data first became available for the country.
