# Country: Chad

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Conflict in the Lake Chad basin, intercommunal violence and disasters were the main triggers of internal displacement in Chad in 2021. Conflict and violence triggered 42,000 new movements, fewer than the 79,000 recorded in 2020. Around 367,000 people were living in displacement as of the end of the year, 392,000 as a result of conflict and violence and 24,000 as a result of disasters.


The number of IDPs in Chad has grown consistently in recent years. The scale and frequency of non-state armed groups’ attacks against civilians has decreased, but the threat remains. Nearly half of displacements in Lac province in 2021, or 17,000 movements, took the form of preventive movements as people fled for fear of attack.


Armed clashes between troops from Chad and the Central African Republic near a border post in Logone Oriental province triggered 4,900 displacements in late May, and intercommunal violence in Salamat province led to 1,500 between January and April. Livelihoods were also destroyed in the Salamat violence and 149 people were killed.


Seasonal rains and floods caused extensive damage to homes, disrupted people’s livelihoods and triggered around 24,000 disaster displacements in 2021. Flooding in Mandoul province in mid-July accounted for more than 80 per cent of the total. IDPs are particularly exposed and vulnerable to extreme weather events, especially those living in protracted displacement. Information gaps hamper a comprehensive understanding of the longer-term impacts of disaster displacement in the country.
