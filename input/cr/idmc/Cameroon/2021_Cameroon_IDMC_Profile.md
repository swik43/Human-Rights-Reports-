# Country: Cameroon

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Cameroon hosts the second largest population of IDPs in Central Africa after the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Their number rose consistently from 2014 to reach an all-time high for the country of more than a million in late 2020. The vast majority fled violence in the Northwest and Southwest regions. Floods, landslides and other sudden-onset disasters also force tens of thousands of people from their homes each year. The country’s crisis is often described one of the world’s most neglected. The humanitarian response is significantly underfunded, with serious implications for IDPs and their host communities.


Conflict and violence triggered 131,000 internal displacements in 2021, up from the 123,000 recorded in 2020. Around 60 per cent took place in the Northwest and Southwest regions, where protests in late 2016 over the long-standing grievances of the French-speaking country’s English-speaking minority escalated into violence between non-state armed groups (NSAGs) and the Cameroonian military.  Over and above funding shortfalls, insecurity has made humanitarian access almost impossible and families have been forced into pendular displacements, compelled to flee their homes and return numerous times in a matter of just a few weeks.


The country’s Far North region has also been affected by conflict and violence, driven by the decade-long insurgency of Boko Haram and the presence of other NSAGs in the Lake Chad basin. More than 13,000 displacements were reported in 2021, including families who fled their homes numerous times, in some cases even every day, for fear of attack. Families and communities whose homes were destroyed faced the prospect of prolonged displacement.


Intercommunal violence in the Far North district of Logone Birni also increased in August and December, prompting more than 40,000 displacements, both cross-border movements to neighbouring Chad and internal movements within Cameroon.


Seasonal floods triggered 1,800 displacements in 2021, significantly fewer than in previous years - a trend also reflected in neighbouring west African countries.


Around 909,000 people were living in displacement in Cameroon as a result of conflict and violence at the end of the year, and around 28,000 as a result of disasters.
