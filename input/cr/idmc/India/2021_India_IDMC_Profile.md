# Country: India

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

India records some of the highest numbers of new displacements in the world every year, the vast majority of them triggered by disasters. The scale of displacement is a result of the country’s exposure to intense and frequent hazards, its large population and socio-economic vulnerability. Disasters led to approximately 4.9 million displacements in 2021, and political and other forms of violence, 13,000.


Floods and cyclones, which are influenced by the south-west and north-east monsoon seasons, are the main trigger of disaster displacement. The south-west monsoon has its onset over the Andaman Sea in late May, bringing heavy rainfall and flooding until September. Around 1.9 million displacements were recorded between June and October 2021, and the north-east monsoon, which runs from October to December, triggered further smaller-scale displacements, primarily in southern states.


Both monsoons drive cyclones that generally occur near the beginning of each season. Three such storms triggered most of the displacement recorded in 2021. Cyclone Tauktae made landfall in Gujarat on 17 May. Tied for the most powerful storm in the state’s history, it prompted 258,000 evacuations and caused destruction across five states and union territories. Cyclone Yaas began to form in the Bay of Bengal less than a week later and triggered a further 2.2 million which had been devastated by Cyclone Amphan in May 2020. More than 50,000 evacuations were organised in Andhra Pradesh and Odisha on 26 September as cyclone Gulab made landfall.


The number of displacements that cyclones trigger has increased in recent years and particularly since 2016. This is in part the result of authorities’ improved evacuation procedures before storms make landfall, and in part because climate change is increasing ocean temperatures and driving more frequent and intense events.


Climate change is also affecting monsoon patterns; extended and erratic seasons are becoming the norm. The 2021 south-west monsoon did not completely withdraw until 25 October, one of the latest dates on record. This coincided with the onset of the north-east monsoon, causing widespread displacement in southern India, which received 60 per cent more rain than usual in October and November. Flooding in Tamil Nadu triggered 312,000 displacements in November.


There is no systematic data collection on displacement triggered by political or intercommunal violence in India, but we recorded 13,000 such displacements in 2021. Post-electoral violence in West Bengal in May prompted 11,000 displacements. Thousands of people also fled intercommunal violence in Tripura and Jammu and Kashmir in July and October, respectively.


Protracted conflict displacement is a challenge in India. There were efforts in 2021 to prioritise resettlement for some IDPs, but at least 506,000 were still living in displacement at the end of the year. Many were displaced decades ago by conflicts in the states of Assam, Mizoram and Tripura in the north-east and Jammu and Kashmir have made little, if any, progress toward achieving durable solutions.
