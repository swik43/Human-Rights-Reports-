# Country: Myanmar (Burma)

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Armed conflict and intercommunal violence have triggered internal displacement in Myanmar for decades, but there was a significant increase in 2021 when more than 448,000 new movements were recorded. The figure is the highest ever for the country and more than the last eight years combined, the result of the aftermath of a military takeover in February which overthrew the country’s democratically elected government.


The armed forces cracked down on protests, clashes between troops and civilians became increasingly violent, and ethnic conflicts in Kachin, northern Shan and Kayin states escalated, all of which led to displacement. Local militias known as People’s Defence Forces also clashed with the military in areas relatively unaffected by conflict displacement in the past. There were reports of displacement from urban centres including Yangon, Mandalay and Ayeyarwady, but figures were difficult to verify.


In Rakhine state, by contrast, a ceasefire agreed in November 2020 led to a lull in fighting between the military and the Arakan Army. That meant fewer new movements were recorded in 2021, but 73,500 people were still living in protracted displacement as a result of the conflict at the end of the year.


The ebb and flow of conflict and violence in Myanmar has reduced IDPs’ resilience, disrupted their livelihoods and exposed them to protection risks. Around 649,000 people were living in displacement as a result of this insecurity at the end of the year, many of whom have been doing so for decades and rely on humanitarian assistance to survive. Their living conditions and pursuit of durable solutions have been further undermined by a lack of documentation, an economic crisis and a deteriorating health system.


Myanmar is also prone to disasters, particularly as a result of weather-related hazards during the monsoon season. Events tend to be small in scale, leading to fewer than 100 displacements at a time, but data from 2008 to 2020 shows two significant peaks. Cyclone Nargis triggered 2.25 million displacements in 2008, and a combination of cyclone Komen and monsoon flooding 1.6 million in 2015.


Disasters triggered more than 158,000 displacements in 2021, and monsoon floods hit areas such as Kayin and Mon states in the south-east, Magway and Sagaing regions in the north-west and Rakhine state in the west, all of which are also affected by conflict. The flooding forced some people already displaced to flee again, aggravating their needs and heightening the risk of their falling into a downward spiral of poverty and vulnerability.


Around 1,400 people were living in displacement as a result of disasters at the end of the year, but the figure should be considered an underestimate.
