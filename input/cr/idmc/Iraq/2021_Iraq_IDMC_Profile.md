# Country: Iraq

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Iraq has a long history of internal displacement rooted in intercommunal tensions and fed by almost two decades of conflict, including the recent war against the Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant (ISIL). The country is also prone to hazards including floods, drought and earthquakes that force people from their homes.


Conflict and violence triggered 57,000 displacements in 2021. Increased ISIL activities and attacks accounted for the majority, particularly in Diyala governorate where many families were displaced for several months between July and October. Intercommunal tensions also led to displacement, as did the Turkish military operations against the Kurdistan Workers Party (PKK). Airstrikes carried out by the Turkish armed forces in the northern Kurdistan region and Ninewa governorate, and counterattacks by the PKK, forced people to flee, but precise figures were hard to come by.


Floods, wildfires and drought triggered 9,400 displacements, a significant increase on the 1,200 recorded in 2020. The country suffered its worst drought in 40 years, and increases in water and food prices had a disproportionate effect on those displaced. This was particularly the case in the largely agricultural governorate of Ninewa, where IDPs and returnees struggled to grow crops and had few if any alternative sources of income or options to sustain themselves.


The closure of displacement camps that began in August 2019 has played a significant role in shifting movement patterns among those affected by conflict. The overwhelming majority of returnees have gone back to their homes, but about half live in inadequate conditions which has played a role in driving secondary movements. The number of returning IDPs fell from an average of around 500,000 a year between 2018 and 2020 to 121,000 in 2021.


Greater efforts to improve security and provide missing civil documents, adequate housing, public services and livelihood opportunities are needed to make returns sustainable. Around 1.2 million people were living in displacement across the country as of the end of the year, the vast majority in informal settings outside camps.
