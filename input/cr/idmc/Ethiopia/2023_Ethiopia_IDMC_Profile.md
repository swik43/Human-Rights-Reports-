# Country: Ethiopia

---

## Year: 2023

### Country Profile

Ethiopia recorded 794,000 displacements by conflict and violence in 2023, down from two million in 2022. A peace agreement between the government and the Tigray People’s Liberation Front (TPLF) in November 2022 enabled better humanitarian access and allowed hundreds of thousands of people to return in 2023. IDPs’ needs were still high, however, particularly for food, in part the result of a locust infestation in August 2023.


While the security situation improved in Tigray, a new wave of violence erupted in the neighbouring region of Amhara, triggering around 407,000 displacements, almost half the national total. Access constraints hampered data collection in the area, however, making this figure likely conservative. Around 140,000 displacements were also reported in the Oromia region, mostly the result of fighting between government forces and the Oromo Liberation Army.


Around 2.9 million people were living in internal displacement as a result of conflict and violence at the end of 2023, down from the 3.9 million reported in 2022. Figures for Tigray reduced by nearly half, but the region was still home to 949,000 IDPs, the largest proportion of the country’s total. Oromia, Somali and Amhara were also hosting significant numbers of IDPs.


Ethiopia also suffered severe floods in 2023. Fuelled in large part by the onset of El Niño, these triggered 550,000 displacements in the country. The floods were preceded by six consecutive failed rainy seasons and drought as a result of La Niña, which affected pastoral and agricultural livelihoods and fuelled a food security crisis, including among IDPs.


Most took place during the rainy season Deyr in southern and south-eastern areas, where rainfall was 300 per cent higher than average. The Somali region was among the most affected, but displacements also occurred in South Ethiopia, South West Ethiopia, Oromia, Afar, Amhara and Gambela regions. The floods also fuelled a cholera outbreak, including among IDPs, particularly in the Amhara and Somali regions. Disasters left an estimated 881,000 people living in internal displacement as of the end of 2023, a 23 per cent increase from 2022 and the third highest figure globally.
