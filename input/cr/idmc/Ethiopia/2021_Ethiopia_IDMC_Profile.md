# Country: Ethiopia

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Conflict and violence triggered more than 5.1 million new displacements in Ethiopia in 2021, three times the number in 2020 and the highest annual figure ever recorded for a single country. The conflict in the northern region of Tigray deepened, spreading to neighbouring regions and uprooting millions of people from their homes.


Conflict between the Ethiopian armed forces and the Tigray People’s Liberation Front (TPLF) broke out in November 2020. The military took control of most of the region in the first weeks of the fighting, and much of the conflict then moved to rural areas of central, southern and western Tigray, triggering 1.8 million displacements. Conflict dynamics shifted in the second half of 2021 after federal forces declared a unilateral ceasefire in June and together with their allies retreated from most of Tigray. The region, however, remained under a de facto humanitarian blockade while widespread violations of international human rights and humanitarian law by all parties to the conflict were reported.


In an effort to reopen aid channels and recapture territory lost in the conflict, the TPLF began to move south and then into the Amhara and Afar regions, where fighting triggered two million displacements. The TPLF announced in mid-December that its forces would retreat to their home region, which allowed hundreds of thousands of people to return to their places of origin in Amhara and Afar. The conflict in the north accounted for most of the displacements recorded in Ethiopia in 2021, but violence in other regions also triggered a significant number. Renewed tensions between regional security forces and non-state armed groups linked to land disputes and competition over resources intensified, sparking deadly clashes that forced people from their homes along the borders between the Afar and Somali regions, the Southern Nations, Nationalities and People's Region (SNNP) and Gambela, and SNNP and Oromia. Intercommunal violence within those regions, and in Benishangul-Gumuz, also led to displacement.


Disasters, mostly floods and drought, triggered 240,000 internal displacements in 2021. The most significant events were the Belg season rains in April and May, which triggered over 170,000 displacements across Afar, Oromia, Somali and SNNP. Drought triggered 54,000 in Afar, Oromia and Somali, more than twice the figure reported in 2020.


Across the country as a whole 4.2 million people were living in displacement at the end of the year, more than 80 per cent of them as a result of conflict and violence. The humanitarian situation remained dire. Displaced people and host communities had very limited access to basic services such as healthcare, water and education, a situation aggravated by the widespread destruction of public and private infrastructure.
