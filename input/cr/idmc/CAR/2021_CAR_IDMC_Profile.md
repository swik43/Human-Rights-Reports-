# Country: Central African Republic

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

The political and security situation in the Central African Republic (CAR) deteriorated significantly at the end of 2020, leading to a sharp rise in conflict displacements in 2021. Around 496,000 were recorded, compared with 318,000 in 2020. Most took place in the prefectures and sub-prefectures of Ouham (Kabo), Ouham-Pendé (Bozoum, Bocaranga, Paoua), Vakaga (Ouanda-Djallé), Haute-Kotto (Bria), Nana-Mambéré (Abba), and Basse-Kotto (Alindao), as well as the capital, Bangui.


The number of internal displacements, some of which were short-term and preventive, peaked at 67,000 in February, the result of coordinated armed attacks by the Coalition of Patriots for Change (Coalition des Patriotes pour le Changement, CPC), which emerged after the December 2020 elections, and a government-led counteroffensive in the north-west and south-east and particularly in Bangassou, Bouar, Grimari and Bangui.


The figures then decreased between March and June before rising again to 60,110 in July and 58,826 in October before tailing off towards the end of the year. The fluctuations are explained in part by military efforts to counter non-state armed groups (NSAGs), which only succeeded in securing a tenuous hold on recaptured territory. Given that several NSAGs constituent of the CPC are signatories to the country’s 2019 peace agreement, CAR’s president declared a unilateral ceasefire in October in an effort to revive the process.


There were 692,000 people living in displacement as a result of conflict and violence at the end of December 2021, a slight increase from the previous year.


CAR also recorded 24,000 disaster displacements in 2021, compared with 15,000 in 2020. Most were triggered by heavy rains and flooding that particularly affected Ombella-Mpoko prefecture and Bangui in August and September. There were 36,000 people living in displacement as a result of disasters across the country at the end of the year.
