# Country: Syria

---

## Year: 2022

### Country Profile

Syria is home to the largest number of internally displaced people globally, with 6.9 million IDPs as of the end of 2022, out of which 6.7 million were displaced by conflict and violence. Most of them have been living in protracted displacement in the north-west, especially since 2013 and 2014 when there was a significant rise in violence between government forces and different non-state armed groups, including the Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant (ISIL), which led to a sharp increase in displacement figures.


In 2022, conflict, violence and disasters continued to trigger displacement, which took place against the backdrop of a worsening social and economic crisis. In terms of conflict and violence, approximately 171,000 internal displacements were recorded throughout the year. More than half of them took place in the northern governorate of Idlib and another 23 per cent in the governorate of Aleppo. This is the lowest figure recorded since 2011. However, such reduction in displacements did not translate in a reduction of the total number of IDPs at the end of 2022, a figure which actually slightly increased from 2021, reflecting the limited options that IDPs still have to sustainably return, resettle, or integrate locally.


Every year, heavy rainfall, strong winds and snowstorms tend to hit the country during the winter months, and despite the cyclical and predictable nature of these hazards, IDPs continue to lack appropriate shelter and mitigation measures, leading to repeated displacement.


In the first quarter of 2022, snowstorms and severe winter conditions destroyed tents in IDP camps in Aleppo and Idlib governorates, triggering more than 13,000 displacements. While the humanitarian community has made progress in building dignified shelters for IDPs living in camps, there is not enough funding to support the restoration of housing, infrastructure and basic services that they need if they are to achieve durable solutions.
