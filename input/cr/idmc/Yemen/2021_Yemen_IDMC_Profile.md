# Country: Yemen (North Yemen)

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

The humanitarian crisis in Yemen remains one of the most acute in the world. As of the end of 2021, around 20.7 million people needed humanitarian assistance across the country, many of whom are suffering from severe food insecurity. An estimated 4.3 million people remained internally displaced across the country, and internal displacement remained unabated during the year. Many IDPs continued to suffer from repeated displacement, often due to a combination of conflict and disasters.


The ongoing conflict affecting the country can be traced back to long-standing political, religious, economic, and social tensions, particularly between the north and the south. In 2011, during the Arab Spring, widespread political unrest, instability and violence triggered about 175,000 internal displacements. Conflict between Ansar Allah, also known as the Houthi movement, and the government grew in intensity, and, in late 2014 ,the group captured Yemen’s capital, Sana’a. In response, a coalition of Arab states led by Saudi Arabia launched a military campaign in 2015 to reinstate the ousted government.


As the war entered its seventh year in 2021, the economic situation deteriorated, the conflict escalated, and thousands of people were killed and injured. More than 377,000 internal displacements associated with conflict and violence were reported during the year, as frontlines shifted and hostilities intensified in Marib, Al Jawz, Shabwah, Taizz and Al Hodeidah. In Marib, some sites experienced a ten-fold increase in the number of IDPs between October and December.


In terms of disasters, an additional 84,000 people were displaced by heavy rains and floods. As of the end of the year, 11,000 remained displaced waiting for their houses and shelters to be rebuilt.


Across the country, many have been repeatedly displaced by conflict, violence and disasters, in a cycle of secondary and protracted displacement. Insecurity, natural hazards, an economic crisis and food insecurity have stalled their progress towards durable solutions.
