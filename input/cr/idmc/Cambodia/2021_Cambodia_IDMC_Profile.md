# Country: Cambodia (Kampuchea)

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Cambodia is highly prone to floods, storms and landslides that cause damage and destruction every year. Most internal displacement is the result of floods during the May to October rainy season, and it takes place in provinces along the Mekong river and around the Tonle Sap lake such as Banteay Menachey, Battambang and Pursat.


Disasters triggered 15,000 new movements in 2021, down from the 66,000 recorded in 2020. A severe lack of rainfall in the Mekong river basin between 2019 and 2021 partly explains the decrease, but floods still accounted for 62 per cent of the disaster displacement recorded while the other 38 percent was a result of storms.


The most significant event was flash flooding in the Dangrek mountains in September that triggered at least 7,000 displacements in the provinces of Banteay Meanchey, Battambang, Kampong Speu, Preah Vihear and Ratanakiri. Across the country as a whole around 5,800 people were living in displacement as a result of disasters at the end of the year. 


The Cambodian government has taken steps in recent years to improve its approach to disaster risk reduction. These include setting up flood risk management policies and frameworks, and a national disaster loss database to monitor progress toward targets set out in the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction. 


Climate change is expected to increase the frequency and intensity of weather-related hazards in the country. This combined with rapid urbanisation and the damming of the Mekong river heighten the risk of both floods and drought. Such events will undoubtedly continue to trigger disaster displacement, which calls for greater efforts to monitor the phenomenon.
