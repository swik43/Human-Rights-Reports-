# Country: DR Congo (Zaire)

---

## Year: 2021

### Country Profile

Conflict and violence triggered 2.7 million internal displacements in DRC’s eastern provinces of Ituri, North Kivu, and South Kivu and the Kasai region in 2021, up from the 2.2 million recorded for the country in 2020. People fled military operations against non-state armed groups (NSAGs), clashes between groups and intercommunal violence. IDPs’ living conditions also deteriorated in some parts of the country. As of the end of the year, 5,339,000 people remained displaced due to armed conflict and violence.


DRC’s president, Felix Tshisekedi, declared martial law in North Kivu and Ituri from 6 May, and put the conflicts in the eastern provinces on the agenda of the African Union, which is a guarantor of the 2013 Peace, Security and Cooperation Framework for the DRC and the region, covering these conflicts, thereby ensuring they remain a priority. Martial law was still in place at the end of the year as the country’s military and the UN stabilisation mission in DRC (MONUSCO) continued to their efforts to combat the numerous NSAGs present. Results, however, have been mixed. Violence has not abated and human rights abuses have continued.


Disasters triggered more than 888,000 internal displacements in 2021, compared with 279,000 in 2020. Mount Nyiragongo near Goma city in North Kivu erupted in May, destroying 3,629 homes and accounting for almost 600,000 movements. It also led to several thousand cross-border movements into Rwanda and to a lesser extent Uganda. As of mid-December, 30,253 people were still internally displaced as a result of the eruption, which came on top of prevailing insecurity in the province.


Torrential rains and floods between January and April destroyed 43,319 homes and triggered more than 200,000 displacements, the vast majority in Tanganyika province. More than 1,100 hectares of farmland were also affected, heightening food insecurity in Kabalo territory in particular. Around 217,000 people were living in internal displacement as of the end of the year having lost their homes to heavy rains, strong winds, floods and the volcanic eruption.
