# Country: DR Congo (Zaire)

---

## Year: 2023

### Country Profile

Conflict and violence triggered 3.8 million displacements in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) in 2023, a slight fall from the record four million in 2022, but still the second-highest figure globally after Sudan. DRC has been among the two countries most affected by conflict displacement globally since 2016.


A large number of non-state armed groups (NSAGs), in particular the March 23 Movement (M23), the Allied Democratic Forces (ADF) and the Cooperative for Development of the Congo (CODECO), continued to operate in the eastern provinces of North Kivu, Ituri and South Kivu, conducting attacks against civilians, including IDPs.


The government made efforts to restore peace and security, including through the deployment of the East African Community Regional Force in November 2022. Angola and the African Union also facilitated a ceasefire between the government and the M23 in March 2023, leading to the group’s withdrawal from some areas and a slight lull in fighting and displacement. The ceasefire was generally respected until October, when violence increased again in North Kivu and triggered more than 821,000 displacements in two months.


The province has recorded the highest figures in DRC since M23’s resurgence in November 2021, and accounted for more than half of the national total for conflict in 2023, with almost two million movements. Most took place in the same hotspots as previous years, particularly the territories of Masisi and Rutshuru. Many people fled to the provincial capital of Goma and its surroundings.


Disputes over land and resources fuelled an escalation in conflict and displacement in neighbouring Ituri, where approximately a million displacements were recorded. Many involved the repeated movement of people fleeing clashes between NSAGs. The violence also undermined the livelihoods of millions of people who rely on subsistence agriculture, which aggravated food insecurity in the province. South Kivu recorded at least 330,000 movements, mostly the result of localised clashes between NSAGs.


Around 6.7 million people were living in displacement as a result of conflict and violence at the end of 2023. This is the highest figure ever reported for the country, partly because of wider coverage in data collection. Fifty-two per cent of IDPs were in North Kivu, South Kivu and Ituri.


In terms of disasters, 133,000 internal displacements were recorded in 2023, the lowest figure since 2018. Many of them were recorded in the east of the country as floods and storms affected South Kivu in November and December
