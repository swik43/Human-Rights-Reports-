# Country: Mali

---

## Year: 2023

### Country Profile

Conflict and violence triggered 152,000 displacements in Mali in 2023, a figure similar to 2022 but which should be considered conservative given access restrictions in many areas. In June, the government request the decade-long UN peacekeeping operation, MINUSMA, to leave the country. Increased insecurity prompted the peacekeepers to accelerate their withdrawal, which was completed in December. Fighting intensified in the second half of 2023 between government forces and non-state armed groups (NSAGs), as well as between these groups, some of which were signatories of the 2015 peace agreement.


Throughout 2023, almost 85 per cent of displacements were reported in the northern and central regions of Goa, Menaka, Mopti and Tombouktou. Some villages in those regions were blockaded by NSAGs. Other communities pre-emptively moved due to increased insecurity. Additionally, attacks and clashes in the south, including the capital Bamako, saw a rise compared to previous years, as NSAGs expanded their operations. Humanitarian access constraints due to insecurity increased steadily between June and October, hampering access to communities in need and the collection of data on new displacements.


Overall, IDPs faced greater needs than their non-displaced counterparts. Many lost their income as a result of their displacement, which hindered their ability to sustain themselves. Almost two-thirds of internally displaced households lacked access to stable income sources in 2023, compared to less than 20 per cent of non-displaced households. The proportion of internally displaced households lacking adequate housing was five times higher than that of non-displaced households.


At the end of 2023, around 344,000 people were internally displaced because of conflict and violence, a slight decrease from the figure for 2022, partly due to improved security in some areas within the above-mentioned regions of the country which allowed some IDPs to return. The figure is still the second highest recorded in the country since 2012.


Like many countries in West Africa, the collection of disaster displacement data in Mali has been hindered by persistent conflict and violence. Such data has been scarce since 2021, mostly because of access constraints and the focus of data collection by governments and their humanitarian partners on informing their responses to the impacts of conflict. The 1,300 flood-related displacements recorded throughout 2023 took place in the region of Mopti. Around 8,000 people were living in displacement at the end of 2023 due to disasters.
